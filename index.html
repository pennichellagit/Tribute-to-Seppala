Leonhard Seppala

1877 - 1967

Leonhard "Sepp" Seppala (1877-1967) was a renowned sled dog breeder, trainer, and musher. He played a crucial role in the 1925 serum run to Nome and competed in the 1932 Winter Olympics. Seppala introduced the Siberian Husky breed to the American public. The prestigious Leonhard Seppala Humanitarian Award recognizes his contributions to sled dog care.

1925 serum run to Nome

Seppala played a pivotal role in the historic "Great Race of Mercy" during the diphtheria outbreak in Nome, Alaska in 1925.
As a skilled musher, Seppala was selected for a dangerous dog sled relay to deliver life-saving diphtheria antitoxin serum to the town. He faced daunting challenges, including treacherous ice on Norton Sound and extreme windchill.
Undeterred, Seppala fearlessly led his team across the perilous terrain, covering over 400 miles in freezing temperatures.
Despite exhaustion and blizzard conditions, he successfully handed over the serum to the next musher, bringing it closer to Nome.
Seppala's heroic efforts contributed significantly to the successful delivery of the serum, ultimately saving numerous lives in Nome.
His unwavering determination and exceptional skills as a musher continue to be celebrated through the annual Iditarod Trail Sled Dog Race, honoring his enduring legacy.
